I work in the game design industry as a QA Analyst. A major part of my role
involves identifying grammar issues and reporting bugs. My work is consistent
in this way: I point out the initial error, and I also include
practical solutions using out‑of‑the‑box thinking that helps developers
understand two things: what went wrong, and how it can be fixed.

This approach saves time and maintains a positive and collaborative atmosphere.
In this way, I’m able to alleviate the negativity that can sometimes come
with the editing or debugging process, turning feedback into progress.

CREATIVE PROCESS


The Creation of Mad House


I was inspired to create a game based on my own personal experiences about
a moment of my life in which I felt like it was so bad-it couldn't possibly be real.
I wanted to tell a story to inspire others that the death of something can be a
chance for hope, peace, and a new beginning. And that the goodness in life comes from
a strong will, hard work, and determination.

I was inspired by Mortis Ghost's OFF, and Persona 3.



I also took inspiration from music, pinpointing lyrics and feelings that represented
a loss of hope, and a rediscovery of the self.

Final Product



Mad House opens in the hollow, which is a workplace meant for people,
yet strangely devoid of life. The protagonist enters as just another
new hire, drifting through routines that seem to echo without meaning.
Then, something unexpected happens. Through small moments shared with
his coworkers, he begins to grow roots. Their presence awakens a will
he didn’t know he had.

This is a story about a newfound resolve becoming a flame, urging him
towards a choice that terrifies him precisely because it matters.
When he steps forward, choosing a path that no one else dares to venture,
the audience feels that tug in their chest. I believe that in his courage,
we see the version of ourselves we hope to become. Brave,
compassionate, and standing tall. Deep down, I believe we all yearn
to experience those moments in our everyday life.

I was recently interviewed for an editorial about my experience with the game
Anodyne for Nintendo Switch, and how it intersected with my life during the
COVID‑19 pandemic. I shared details about navigating the challenges of modern-day
life, isolation and uncertainty. The game became an unexpected space for
self reflection and emotional grounding.



MUSIC




My piano rendition of Ode to Heroes captures the emotional core
of Metaphor: ReFantazio in a beautifully personal way. Recorded in Logic Pro
with my MIDI keyboard and layered with a soft, atmospheric hum of a washing machine,
it takes on that raw indie producer vibe seen in European indie cover uploads.
The track itself plays during the game’s most heartfelt moments that blend fantasy
and reality. This theme defines the entire story. This version gives it a grounded
and emotional human touch.




My original piece "Winter With You" from our game Himawari.
This is a showcase of Logic Pro on Apple Macbook Pro.
The song conveys a feeling like snow.





3/31 is a definitive song from the game Persona 3 in which our main character
is struggling through a tough moment in her life after experiencing loss.
It's gritty and unnatural to the ear. A great piece to keep your attention.
My rendition layers the original with a bit more dusk. My thought process
with this was to mirror the feelings of diving into this game's dungeon
that only appears at midnight, called the Dark Hour.
